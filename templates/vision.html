Problem statement: Food security and zero hunger

Hunger is not just about scarcity. It’s driven by poor crop predictions, food waste, and inefficient distribution. Globally, 30–40% of food is lost between harvest and consumption—1.3 billion tons annually.

In Sub-Saharan Africa, post-harvest losses reach 37% for fruits and vegetables due to inadequate storage, poor transportation, and lack of real-time quality monitoring—representing $4 billion in losses and affecting 282 million people.

Core gaps for small-scale farmers

  • Predictive analytics: Limited access to yield and risk predictions
  • Quality monitoring: No continuous tracking of storage conditions
  • Market access: Weak connections to buyers in shortage areas

Proposed solution overview

Use real-time weather, soil, and satellite data. Apply AI to predict crop yield and detect risks (drought, pests, floods). Provide role-based dashboards for farmers, suppliers, and governments. Connect surplus producers with shortage areas to reduce waste.

  • IoT sensors: Temperature, humidity, CO₂ monitoring
  • AI analytics: IBM Z-powered models for harvest timing and storage duration
  • Blockchain: Immutable farm-to-market tracking for safety and fraud reduction
  • Dynamic matching: Real-time farmer–buyer connections based on quality predictions

Dynamic Market Matching identifies buyers for surplus lots using quality predictions, distance, and price tolerance—reducing waste while optimizing logistics.

SMS/app alerts deliver storage condition warnings, market opportunities, and weather-based recommendations via a low-bandwidth progressive web app for remote areas.
